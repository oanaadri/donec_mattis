Experience

2018 - 2012: Career beginnings at Nickelodeon

By age 13, Grande became serious about pursuing a music career, although she still concentrated on theater. When she first arrived in Los Angeles, California to meet with her managers, she expressed a desire to record an R&B album: "I was like, 'I want to make an R&B album,' They were like 'Um, that's a helluva goal! Who is going to buy a 14-year-old's R&B album?!'" In 2008, Grande was cast as cheerleader Charlotte in the Broadway musical 13. When she joined the musical, Grande left North Broward Preparatory School, but continued to be enrolled; the school sent her materials to study with tutors. She also sang various times at the New York City jazz club Birdland.

2013–2015: Yours Truly and My Everything

Grande recorded her debut studio album Yours Truly, originally titled Daydreamin', over three years.[65] It was released on August 30, 2013, and debuted at number one on the US Billboard 200 albums chart, with 138,000 copies sold in its first week.[66][67] Yours Truly also debuted in the top ten in several other countries, including Australia,[68] the UK,[69] Ireland,[70] and the Netherlands.[71] Its lead single, "The Way", featuring Pittsburgh rapper Mac Miller, debuted at number ten on the US Billboard Hot 100,[72] eventually peaking at number nine for two weeks.[73] Grande was later sued by Minder Music for copying the line "What we gotta do right here is go back, back in time" from the 1972 song "Troglodyte (Cave Man)" by The Jimmy Castor Bunch.[74] The album's second single, "Baby I", was released in July.[75] Its third single, "Right There", featuring Detroit rapper Big Sean, was released in August 2013.[76] They respectively peaked at number 21 and 84 on the Billboard Hot 100.

2015–2017: Dangerous Woman

Grande began recording songs for her third studio album, Dangerous Woman, originally titled Moonlight, in 2015.[114][115] In October of that year, she released the single "Focus", initially intended as the lead single from the album; the song debuted at number seven on the Billboard Hot 100.[116] The next month American singer Who Is Fancy released the single "Boys Like You", which features her and Meghan Trainor.[117] She was featured in the remix version of "Over and Over Again", a song by English singer Nathan Sykes' debut studio solo album Unfinished Business, which was released in January 2016.[118] Grande made a cameo appearance in the comedy film Zoolander 2 starring Ben Stiller and Owen Wilson.[119] In March 2016, Grande released "Dangerous Woman" as the lead single from the retitled album of the same name.[120][121] The single debuted at number ten on the Billboard Hot 100, becoming the first artist to have the lead single from each of her first three albums debut in the top ten.[122] The same month, Grande appeared as host and musical guest of Saturday Night Live, where she performed "Dangerous Woman" and debuted the promotional single "Be Alright",[123] which charted at number 43 on the Billboard Hot 100.[124] Grande garnered positive reviews for her appearance on the show, including praise for her impressions of various singers,[125][126] some of which she had done on The Tonight Show.[127] Grande won an online voting poll on Entertainment Weekly as the "best host of the season".[128] In May 2016, Grande appeared on The Voice season 10 finale, performing the second single from the album, "Into You", which peaked at number 13 in the United States,[129] and duetted with Christina Aguilera on "Dangerous Woman".
